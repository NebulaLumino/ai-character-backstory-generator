# AI Agents in Educational Assessment

## Overview
Assessment is the process of measuring what learners know and can do. Traditional assessment is time-consuming, inconsistent across human graders, and limited in frequency. AI is transforming assessment by enabling continuous, granular, and consistent measurement of learning at scale.

## Current AI Applications

### Automated Essay Scoring (AES)
NLP systems that score essays on dimensions including:
- Argument quality and structure
- Evidence use and citation
- Grammar, mechanics, and style
- Alignment with rubric criteria

Systems like ETS's e-rater and Pearson's Intelligent Bilingual Essay Evaluation have been validated against human graders, achieving correlations of 0.85-0.92 with expert human scores.

### Intelligent Examination Systems
- **Computer-adaptive testing**: AI that selects each question based on estimated ability, converging on precise measurement with fewer items
- **Process data analysis**: Mining logs of student behavior during online exams to detect cheating and measure engagement
- **Partial credit modeling**: AI systems that award partial credit for partially correct responses based on fine-grained error analysis

### Formative Assessment
- **Real-time knowledge assessment**: Embedding brief assessment moments within learning activities to measure understanding without disrupting flow
- **Diagnostic assessment**: AI systems that identify specific misconceptions rather than just right/wrong answers
- **Self-assessment support**: AI that helps students accurately evaluate their own work and identify areas for improvement

### Assessment Security
- **Plagiarism detection**: AI systems that compare submitted work against databases of known sources and flag unusual stylistic patterns
- **Proctoring**: Computer vision systems that monitor test-taking environments for suspicious behavior
- **Answer steal detection**: Identifying when answers on one exam are suspiciously similar to answers on another

## Key AI Techniques
- **Rubric-based NLP**: Models trained on rubric-aligned human-scored essays
- **Latent trait models**: Bayesian approaches that infer ability from both answer correctness and response time
- **Process mining**: Analyzing keystrokes, mouse movements, and timing patterns during assessment

## Challenges
**Construct validity**: AI systems optimize for measurable aspects of performance but may miss important unmeasured dimensions.

**Adversarial adaptation**: Students increasingly use AI to generate assessment responses, requiring detection systems to keep pace.

**Fairness and bias**: AI assessment systems trained on historical data can perpetuate biases in grading standards.

## Future Directions
AI portfolios that continuously assess learner work over time, building competency maps that replace high-stakes point-in-time examinations.

Multimodal assessment that combines written, oral, and practical demonstration of competence, evaluated holistically by AI systems.
